Where to Start:


LinkedIn

First thing's first, make a LinkedIn account, if you don't already have one.

LinkedIn is a great resource for networking and connecting with people.

They even have an option to alert Recruiters and Hiring Managers in your network that you are looking for work.


Face-to-Face Networking

This is something that every developer and job-seeker should get involved with, and is one of the best ways to discover job leads that prospective developers often overlook.

Networking with people in your area in your field is a sure-fire way to land a job, and many of these meetups start with a "Who's Hiring?" section specifically for job-seekers.

Remember, it's 10% what you know, and 90% who you know.

Some websites to help you start networking:

Meetup.com
Useful for finding meetups in your area that are specific to the field you are looking for.
Eventbrite.com
Just like Meetup, Eventbrite is useful for networking with people in the same area of expertise that you're looking for.

Reddit

No, Reddit isn't only for wasting time anymore.

Reddit is also great for job-hunters as a one-stop-shop for networking through various IT subjects in the numerous subreddits available. Remember, it's the #1 most trafficked site in the world. There's even a specific subreddit for DFW area job-seekers!
