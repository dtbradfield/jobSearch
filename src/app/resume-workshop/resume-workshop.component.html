How to Write a Clean Resume:

Style Guidlines:
You can find a good sample resume to model yours after here.
 
Font Styles
Font Families:
Arial, Georgia, Calibri or Times New Roman
Font Sizing:

Title: 20pt

Subject Header: 14pt

Body: 10pt


Structure
1. Summary
5 sentences that give an over view of who you are, your total years of experience in the field and then the number of years of specialization. Last sentence should clearly indicate what your next career move is going to be.
2. Skills
Just a list of all of your skills in bullets or table format.
3. Work Experience
  1. Company Name, City and State, and length of time with the company
  2. Title of your Position
  3. Bullet pointed sentences of specific examples of work, technologies used and data. Explain how you contributed.
4. Education
  1. School Name, City and State, Graduation date
  2. Major or Certification
Final Resume Tips:
  1. Make sure your resume looks professional and easy to read. Your resume is a reflection of yourself.
  2. If it looks painful to the eyes then it will end up in the trash.
  3. Yes – bullet points are awesome
  4. No – blocks of text and clutter are bad
  5. If you have gaps of time on your resume then you need to address those and explain them.
    • If you have 3+ months of time where you are no working, getting educated or doing something productive then it will look bad and will be questioned up front and on interview.
